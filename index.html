About Me

Hi! I'm Manishika Balamurugan, a rising third-year at the University of Michigan.

I'm double majoring in Biopsychology, Cognition, and Neuroscience and Computer Science.

My interests lie at the intersection of brain science and computation. I'm passionate about using brain imaging and computational modeling to study neurodegenerative diseases and explore possible pathways for neural repair.

At the University of Michigan, I work in a Computational and Cognitive Neuroscience Lab, where I study neural dedifferentiation in older adults who are either healthy or have a mild cognitive impairment diagnosis.

This summer, I am completing a research fellowship in the Regenerative Imaging Laboratory at the University of Pittsburgh where I'm contributing to the development of a new hippocampus model.

In the near future, I want to refine my coding skills through a software engineering internship. I want to gain experience in a company setting and branch out from research, which I have been focusing on for the last few years.
