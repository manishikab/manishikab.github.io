About Me!

I grew up in Lincoln, Nebraska. I was a young coder, spending hours on Scratch and Code.org. With no computer science curriculum in high school, I turned my focus to another type of computer: the brain.
In my higher education, I strive to integrate these two interests to contribute to meaningful research in the field of brain science.

At the University of Michigan, I work in a Computational and Cognitive Neuroscience Lab, where I study neural dedifferentiation in older adults who are either healthy or have a mild cognitive impairment diagnosis.

This summer, I am completing a research fellowship at the University of Pittsburgh where I'm contributing to the development of a new hippocampus model.

In the near future, I want to refine my coding skills through a software engineering internship. I want to gain experience in a company setting and branch out from research, which I have been focusing on for the last few years.
